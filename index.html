Biography

Hello. I am a MSc student in Computer Science at Université Laval in Canada. My research interests primarily lie in machine learning. More specifically, I am interested in the study of uncertainty using Bayesian and PAC-Bayesian statistical tools.

I am part of the GRAIL research group where I am advised by François Laviolette and Pascal Germain.

Motivation

Deep learning is a emerging field that has seen most of the recent achievements of machine learning. However, its astonishingly rapid expansion has overlooked the theoretical guarantees and properties of neural networks, thus limiting their effective scope. Many real-world applications in the medical and aerospace industries require an understanding of uncertainty, as anticipating mistakes can prevent costly or life-threatening situations.
